Overview

Bacterial blight (BB) is a destructive disease in rice caused by the gram-negative bacterium Xanthomonas oryzae. There are two main pathovars of interest: pv. oryzae (Xoo) and pv. oryzicola. The former enters the rice plant through hydathodes or wounds, multiples in the intercellular spaces of the underlying epitheme, and propagate to reach the xylem vessels. The latter penetrates the leaf through stomata or wounds, multiplies in the substomatal cavity, and colonizes the intercellular spaces of the parenchyma.

Receptor-Like Kinase (RLK) Genes

  • aids in recognizing pathogen-associated molecular patterns (PAMPs)
  • typically contains:
    • extracellular domain
    • single-pass transmembrane domain
    • intracellular kinase domain
  • LRR-RLK gene Xa21 originated from Oryza longistaminata, a wild rice species
    • confers broad-spectrum resistance to Xoo
  • Xa3/Xa26 interacts with OsSERK2 and OsTPI1.1 to mediate resistance
  • Xa4 encodes a cell wall-associated kinase that confers race-specific resistance to Xoo at all stages of rice growth
    • found the almost all indica hybrid rice cultivars in China
    • prevents Xoo invasion by reinforcing the call wall with phytoalexins, sakuranetin, and momilactone A
    • can increase mechanical strength of the culm to enhance lodging tolerance

Sugar Will Eventually be Exported Transporter (SWEET) Genes

  • encode for a family of sugar efflux transporters
  • play a vital role in pollen nutrition, senescence, seed filling, and plant-pathogen interaction
  • there are 22 SWEET genes in rice which are grouped into four clades
  • hijacked by TAL effectors of the pathogen for their growth and virulence
  • xa13 (or Os8N3, OsSWEET11) confers specific resistance to Xoo race 6
    • dominant allele (Xa13) is needed by Xoo (and Rhizoctonia solani) to promote the expression of its TALE effector
    • recessive allele (xa13) does not induce expression
    • also cooperates with copper transporters (COPT1, COPT5) to remove toxic Cu from xylem vessels where the pathogen multiplies and spreads
    • Xa13-silenced plants had low fertility, implicating its role in pollination
  • Xa25 (or OsSWEET13) confers race-specific resistance to Xoo strain PXO339
  • Xa41 (or Os11N3, OsSWEET14) is the susceptibility gene for different Xoo strains

Executor Genes

  • Xa27, Xa10, and Xa23 function as promoter traps which are activated by TALEs and trigger defense responses
    • Xa27 from O. minuta confers broad-spectrum resistance
    • Xa10 confers resistance to some Philippine races
    • Xa23 from O. rufipogon confers extremely broad spectrum resistance at all growth stages of rice
